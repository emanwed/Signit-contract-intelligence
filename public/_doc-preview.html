1PROBLEM ANALYSIS

The pain isn’t reading contracts — it’s the post-signature black hole

The brief describes shared drives, stale spreadsheets and terms discovered after they trigger. The crowded, well-funded side of “contract AI” is pre-signature — drafting, redlining, negotiation. The money actually leaks after signing, where a contract becomes a PDF nobody opens until it hurts. That reframing set our entire scope: Signit begins the moment every other tool stops.

Surface symptomReal underlying painWho feels itCost of doing nothing
“I can’t find that clause”Knowledge trapped in unsearchable bilingual PDFs; keyword search fails on meaning and on Arabic/Franco-Arabic inputLegalHours per question; inconsistent answers
“It auto-renewed again”Notice windows are invisible until they close; no owner, no escalation, no record of what was decidedProcurement / FinanceUnwanted spend & vendor lock-in
“What’s our total exposure?”No aggregate view of commitments, cash due, supplier concentration or compliance postureExecutive / CFORisk that is real but unquantified
“Is the AI even right?”Black-box extraction offers no way to verify — so it’s quietly abandonedEveryoneTool distrust → shelfware
“We marked it done… then what?”Task tools record that something was handled, never what was decided — so the system’s picture of the portfolio drifts from realityEveryoneStale data; the next deadline is wrong
Not obvious #1

Extraction is table-stakes. Trust & action are the product.

Pulling facts out of a contract is a largely solved LLM task. The unsolved problem is getting a non-lawyer to believe a fact, act on it in time, and tell the system what they did so it stays true. We built the UX around that loop, not around documents.

Not obvious #2

“Done” is not an outcome.

Asking “did you handle the renewal?” is useless to a system. Asking “what did you do — renew, renegotiate, or terminate?” lets it schedule the correct next action and keep the portfolio truthful. This became our signature interaction.

Not obvious #3

In KSA, bilingual + Hijri + local law is the product, not polish.

Contracts are Arabic-first and often bilingual; users type Franco-Arabic (“mas2ooleya”); deadlines read in Hijri and Gregorian; the compliance frame is PDPL, ZATCA, Labor Law, Saudization. Retrofitting Arabic onto an English tool fails here.

Assumptions we are making

StageContracts are already executed — post-signature only.
InputsSemi-structured PDFs/text; Arabic, English or bilingual; variable quality.
ScaleTens to a few hundred live contracts per organization.
UsersMostly non-lawyers acting on legal content.
AIExtraction is good, not perfect — the UX must assume the AI is sometimes wrong.

Highest-leverage order

What we solved first, and why.

  1. Trust — without verifiable facts, nothing else gets used.
  2. Never miss an obligation — the clearest, most quantifiable loss.
  3. Close the loop — capture outcomes so the data stays true.
  4. Aggregate exposure — turn portfolio risk into numbers with drill-downs.

2SOLUTION OVERVIEW

Don’t make people read contracts. Make contracts answer, chase, and learn — with receipts.

Signit is a post-signature intelligence layer that lives inside the Signit e-signature product (built into its real two-layer navigation shell). It reads every signed contract once into a confidence-scored model, then serves the same facts to three roles at three altitudes — and runs an action loop that assigns deadlines to teams, records the real-world outcome, and states the next step it schedules. The organizing principle throughout is verifiable confidence.

Four design bets (all live in the prototype)

Bet 1 · Trust

Source-linked confidence

Every extracted fact carries a High/Med/Low confidence pill and links to the exact bilingual source clause. Click any value → see the original text it came from, highlighted inside the full document. Low-confidence facts automatically become “review” actions that chase a human.

Prototype: contract drawer — Values / Document / Risk tabs
Bet 2 · Closed loop

Outcome-logged resolution the “clever” bit

Actions aren’t checked off — they’re resolved with what the company actually did (e.g. a renewal: renew / renegotiate / terminate / defer). The system immediately states the next step it schedules and how the portfolio updates. The audit log records who decided what, when.

Prototype: Action Center → “الإجراء المتّخذ” per category
Bet 3 · Coherence

One dataset, three lenses

A persona switch (Executive / Procurement / Legal) changes altitude, emphasis and scope — Procurement’s Action Center shows only Procurement’s actions — but never the underlying data. One product, not three; the roles can’t disagree on the numbers.

Prototype: persona lens re-frames KPIs + Action Center
Bet 4 · Local-first

Arabic-first, KSA-native

RTL by default with full English switch, Hijri + Gregorian dates, Arabic-Indic numerals, and Franco-Arabic (Arabizi) understood in search and Q&A. Compliance is framed in 10 Saudi regulations (PDPL, ZATCA, Labor Law, Companies Law, Nazaha, Nitaqat, SAIP, NCA, AML, Gov-Tenders) plus the company’s own policy documents.

Prototype: language toggle · Compliance & Policies

In scope — built & working in the prototype

Live AI clause/fact extraction on upload · source-linked confidence · risk gauge + six clause dimensions + playbook comparison · full contract register (content search incl. Franco-Arabic, unified filters, payment cadence) · Action Center: 7 action categories, deadline tiers, team assignment with confirm, outcome logging + next-step, comments & audit trail · 13 configurable compliance checks + company policy documents (upload / preview / apply per-document) · portfolio KPIs per persona with action verbs and drill-downs · Ask Signit multi-turn Q&A with source chips · notifications with per-category prefs · enforced Free vs Pro plan · contract editing · demo reset.

3USER FLOWS & INTERACTION MODEL

The same facts, served at three altitudes

The hard UX problem is multi-persona coherence: a CFO and a lawyer need very different depths from the same contract. We solve it with one confidence-scored dataset, a persona lens, and progressive disclosure — never three separate apps, and never a generic dashboard: every number on screen drills down to the clause that produced it.

PersonaCore questionAltitudeWhat their lens shows (in the prototype)
Executive (CFO/COO)“What are we committed to, and what could bite us this quarter?”PortfolioFive KPI cards, each ending in an action verb + drill-down: total commitments (30.3M ﷼ · trend), cash due in 90 days, value renewing without negotiation, supplier concentration, compliance posture /100
Procurement (Ops)“What’s closing, who owns it, and what did we decide?”ObligationClosing notice windows · renegotiable value · imminent auto-renewals · overdue vendor obligations — and an Action Center scoped to their team’s actions only
Legal (Counsel)“Show me the non-standard, elevated-risk clauses — verified.”ClauseHigh risks awaiting decision · playbook deviations · missing essential clauses · PDPL gaps · low-confidence extractions to verify; per-contract risk gauge + 6 dimensions + clause-vs-playbook table
Principle

Progressive disclosure

KPI number → filtered register → contract drawer → the exact source clause. Each click adds one layer of depth on demand — a CFO stops at the number, a lawyer drills to the clause. Same path, different stopping points.

Principle

Push, don’t pull

Deadlines surface themselves in the Action Center — grouped by urgency (overdue / 7 / 30 days), pre-assigned to a responsible team, with a live badge count in the navigation. Nobody has to remember to look.

Principle

Close the loop

Resolving an action means recording the decision. The system replies with the next step it schedules (“notice sent → handover action created; contract marked ending at term”), so tomorrow’s data reflects today’s decisions.

Altitude — the same contract, read at three depths

Portfolio · Executive — commitments & trend, cash due, renewal exposure, concentration, compliance score.
Obligation · Procurement — the action calendar: 7 categories, urgency tiers, team owners, outcomes and escalation.
Clause · Legal — risk gauge & six dimensions, playbook comparison, and the verified source clause behind every fact.

Edge cases & real-world messiness — designed in, not hand-waved.
When…Signit’s behaviour
The AI is wrongEvery fact is source-linked + confidence-scored, so a wrong value is caught in one click; low-confidence extractions spawn review actions that chase a human, and any field can be edited manually.
A value is missing / ambiguousExplicit “—” and “unconfirmed” states; grounded generation never invents a value, an ID or a figure.
The live model is unavailableAsk degrades gracefully to a local bilingual matcher and says so — the per-answer “demo” badge is part of the trust design.
Formats & languages varyArabic / English / bilingual; RTL-first with full LTR switch; Hijri + Gregorian dates; Franco-Arabic input; uploaded PDFs render as-is, contracts without a file get a reconstructed document with source clauses highlighted.
Destructive / high-stakes movesDeleting a contract or file and reassigning a team all require an explicit two-step confirm; a demo-wide reset restores the seed state.

5WHAT WE’D DO WITH MORE TIME — PHASE 2

P2 · Close the loop for real

Outcomes drive the data

Logged outcomes re-compute KPIs and auto-create the stated next action (today the system declares it). Instrument the success metrics: renewals caught before the window, review-time saved, % facts human-verified.

P2 · Retrieval

Real embeddings + vector store

Clause-level semantic search (pgvector) behind the same UX, plus cross-contract clause comparison (“show all liability caps side-by-side”). The interaction model is already proven; this swaps the engine.

P2 · Channels

Delivery & escalation

Real email / Slack / Teams sending for the 30-7-1-day tiers with manager escalation — owners and channels are already modeled in the UI.

P2 · Ingestion

Messy reality at scale

OCR for scans, batch import from the Signit signing archive, amendment & renewal chains (contract versioning), duplicate detection.

P2 · Enterprise

Lenses become permissions

SSO and role-based access grown from the persona lens; multi-entity roll-ups for groups; exportable audit trail; SOC 2 posture.

P2 · Intelligence

Predictive layer

Negotiation-leverage scoring ahead of renewals, clause benchmarking against market standards, and portfolio-level compliance forecasting.

AI tools used to build this (as the brief requests): the prototype, sample portfolio (31 realistic Saudi contracts), and this document were built end-to-end with Claude Code (Anthropic’s agentic coding tool — Claude Opus/Sonnet) over ~3 days. The product itself calls Claude via the Anthropic API for live extraction and Q&A, with a prompt-caching-optimal layout and graceful offline fallbacks. Stack: Next.js (App Router) · TypeScript · Tailwind v4.
