1PROBLEM ANALYSIS

The pain isn’t reading contracts — it’s the post-signature black hole

The brief asked for “contract intelligence.” The crowded, well-funded part of that market is pre-signature — drafting, redlining, negotiation. The money actually leaks after signing, where the contract becomes a PDF no one opens until something goes wrong. That reframing set our entire scope.

Surface symptomReal underlying painWho feels itCost of doing nothing
“I can’t find that clause”Knowledge is trapped in unsearchable bilingual PDFs; keyword search fails on meaningLegalHours per contract; inconsistent answers
“It auto-renewed again”No obligation tracking — renewal & notice deadlines are invisible until they passProcurement / FinanceUnwanted spend & vendor lock-in
“What’s our total exposure?”No aggregate view of liability, caps or jurisdiction across the portfolioExecutive / CFORisk that is real but unquantified
“Is the AI even right?”Black-box extraction offers no way to verify — so it’s quietly abandonedEveryoneTool distrust → shelfware
Not obvious #1

Extraction is table-stakes. Trust & action are the product.

Pulling facts from a contract is a solved LLM task. The unsolved problem is getting a non-lawyer to believe a fact and act on it in time. We built the UI around that, not around documents.

Not obvious #2

Pre-signature is crowded; post-signature is the leak.

We deliberately ignore drafting/negotiation. Signit starts the moment a contract is signed — the exact window every other tool drops.

Not obvious #3

In KSA, bilingual + Hijri + PDPL is the product, not polish.

Contracts are Arabic-first and often bilingual; deadlines are Hijri; jurisdiction preference is local. Retrofitting Arabic onto an English tool fails here.

2SOLUTION OVERVIEW

Don’t make people read contracts. Make contracts answer questions — with receipts.

Signit is a post-signature intelligence layer that sits inside the Signit e-signature product. It reads every signed contract once, then serves the same underlying facts to three roles at three altitudes. The organising principle throughout is verifiable confidence.

Three design bets

Bet 1 · Trust

Source-linked confidence

Every extracted fact carries a High/Med/Low confidence pill and links to the exact source clause. Click a value → see the original bilingual text it came from.

In prototype: the Drawer (hero)
Bet 2 · Coherence

One dataset, three lenses

A persona switch (Executive / Procurement / Legal) changes altitude and emphasis, never the underlying data — so it’s one product, not three.

In prototype: persona lens + KPIs
Bet 3 · Action

Post-signature action loop

The system pushes: obligations become calendar items, alerts fire at 30 / 7 / 1 days, owners are notified, misses escalate to a manager.

In prototype: Obligations + Action Center

In scope — built in the prototype

Clause & fact extraction · type classification · risk scoring & playbook comparison · obligations & calendar · semantic search · portfolio analytics · Ask Signit (NL Q&A) · PDPL / ZATCA / Labor-law checks · Free vs Pro gating.

3USER FLOWS & INTERACTION MODEL

The same facts, served at three altitudes

The hard UX problem is multi-persona coherence: a CFO and a lawyer need very different depths from the same contract. We solve it with one dataset, a persona lens, and progressive disclosure — never three separate apps.

PersonaCore questionAltitudePrimary Signit surface
Executive (CFO)“What’s our exposure, and what could bite us this quarter?”PortfolioKPI dashboard: total liability exposure · auto-renewal risk (90d) · below-playbook count · jurisdiction mix
Procurement“What’s renewing, who owns it, and can we act before the deadline?”ObligationObligations calendar · Action Center · renewal alerts
Legal“Show me the non-standard, elevated-risk clauses — verified.”ClauseRisk gauge + 6 dimensions · playbook comparison · semantic search · source clauses
Interaction principle

Progressive disclosure

KPI number → filtered list → contract drawer → the exact source clause. Each click adds one layer of depth, on demand — a CFO stops at the number, a lawyer drills to the clause.

Interaction principle

Push, don’t pull

Deadlines and anomalies surface themselves in the Action Center with owners, streams and escalation — the user doesn’t have to remember to look.

Altitude — the same contract, read at three depths

Portfolio · Executive — total liability exposure, auto-renewal risk (90d), below-playbook count, jurisdiction mix.
Obligation · Procurement — the calendar, 30/7/1-day alerts, owners and manager escalation.
Clause · Legal — risk gauge & six dimensions, playbook comparison, and the source clause behind every fact.
